The Symphony of Cricket

Under a sky brushed with amber and gold,
Where stories of courage and passion are told,
A field lies waiting, a canvas so wide,
Where dreams take guard and destinies collide.

The pitch, a narrow strip of fate,
Holds silent battles that time will narrate,
Each crack and grain, each dusty line,
Witness to moments both cruel and divine.

A bowler runs with thunder in stride,
Carrying storms that refuse to hide,
The seam whispers secrets as it cuts through air,
A fleeting blur of hope and despair.

The batsman stands, calm yet aware,
With heart like fire and a warrior’s stare,
Bat raised high like a poet’s pen,
Ready to script glory again and again.

The ball strikes leather, a crack so pure,
An echo that promises something more,
It soars through skies like a fleeting dream,
A moment suspended in sunlight’s gleam.

Cheers erupt like a roaring sea,
A symphony of shared ecstasy,
For in that instant, all hearts unite,
Bound by the rhythm of bat and flight.

Fielders chase with desperate grace,
Every dive a fearless embrace,
Grass-stained jerseys, breaths held tight,
Guarding honor with all their might.

And then the spinner, slow and sly,
With mysteries hidden in every try,
The ball dances, deceives, and dips,
A magician’s trick at the batsman’s hips.

The crowd becomes a living flame,
Each voice a part of the sacred game,
From whispered prayers to thunderous cries,
Hope flickers in a thousand eyes.

Scoreboards tick like the pulse of time,
Each run a note in cricket’s rhyme,
Each wicket fallen, a tale of pain,
Each partnership, a monsoon rain.

From dusty gullies where children play,
With broken bats and endless day,
To grand arenas where legends rise,
Cricket lives beneath all skies.

It is more than a game, more than a sport,
It is courage, patience, a silent court,
Where character stands in open view,
And grit decides what fate will do.

A last over dawns, the air grows still,
Hearts beat louder than any thrill,
Six balls remain, the target near,
Between despair and blazing cheer.

A swing, a miss, a gasp, a shout,
A run snatched quick, a desperate rout,
And then—one moment that defines it all,
A soaring six, beyond the wall.

Victory dances in tearful eyes,
While defeat in quiet dignity lies,
For cricket teaches, both harsh and kind,
The strength of body, the depth of mind.

So let the willow sing its song,
Of rights and wrongs, of weak and strong,
For in every match, in loss or gain,
Cricket flows like an endless rain.

A timeless tale, forever new,
Of battles fought in morning dew,
Where every heart, both young and old,
Finds a story worth being told.
